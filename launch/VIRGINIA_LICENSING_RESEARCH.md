# Virginia Consumer Finance License — Research & Compliance Guide

**Regulator:** Virginia State Corporation Commission (SCC), Bureau of Financial Institutions (BFI)  
**License Type:** Consumer Finance License (Va. Code Title 6.2, Chapter 18)  
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-09

---

## Overview

The Virginia Consumer Finance License authorizes a company to make consumer loans up to the statutory limit ($25,000 for small loans) and collect interest, fees, and charges under Virginia law. It is the primary lending license required for non-depository consumer lenders operating in the Commonwealth of Virginia.

## Application Process (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Pre-Application Preparation
- Register with the **Nationwide Multistate Licensing System (NMLS)** — the company and all control persons (owners, directors, officers) must obtain NMLS IDs.
- Prepare **business formation documents** (articles of incorporation/organization, operating agreement, bylaws).
- Draft **business plan** detailing lending operations, underwriting criteria, compliance policies, and financial projections.
- Secure the **$25,000 surety bond** from a licensed surety company.
- Prepare **financial statements** (audited or reviewed, typically for the most recent fiscal year).
- Designate a **registered agent** in Virginia (if formed out of state).

### Step 2: Submit Application via NMLS
- Log in to NMLS, select the Virginia Consumer Finance License application.
- Upload all required documents (see Document Checklist below).
- Pay the **$750 application fee** via NMLS.
- Pay NMLS processing fees (typically ~$100–150).

### Step 3: Background Checks
- All control persons must submit **fingerprints** for FBI/Criminal background checks.
- Credit reports will be pulled for all control persons.
- Disclosure of any prior regulatory actions, bankruptcies, or litigation.

### Step 4: Regulatory Review (6–8 weeks)
- SCC BFI reviews the application for completeness, financial soundness, and compliance with Virginia law.
- The Bureau may request additional information or clarifications.
- A **desk examination** or **on-site examination** may be conducted.
- The Bureau evaluates:
  - Character and fitness of management
  - Financial condition and capital adequacy
  - Business plan viability
  - Compliance systems and internal controls
  - Fair lending practices

### Step 5: Approval & License Issuance
- If approved, the Bureau issues the Consumer Finance License.
- License is valid until December 31 of the issuance year and must be renewed annually.
- License number is recorded in NMLS.

## Statutory & Regulatory Requirements

### 36% APR Cap — Va. Code § 6.2-1816
Virginia law caps the annual percentage rate (APR) on consumer finance loans at **36%**. This includes:
- Interest
- Fees
- Charges
- Credit insurance premiums
- Any other consideration for the extension of credit

> **Important:** This cap applies to loans made under the Consumer Finance Act. Lenders must ensure their loan products do not exceed this APR threshold.

### Loan Amount Limits
- Maximum loan amount under the Consumer Finance Act: **$25,000**
- Loans exceeding $25,000 may require a different licensing structure or exemption.

### Other Key Statutes
- **Va. Code § 6.2-1800 et seq.** — Consumer Finance Act (complete regulatory framework)
- **Va. Code § 6.2-1815** — License required for lending
- **Va. Code § 6.2-1817** — Licensee recordkeeping requirements
- **Va. Code § 6.2-1820** — Prohibited acts and practices
- **Va. Code § 6.2-1822** — Revocation, suspension, or surrender of license
- **Va. Code § 6.2-400 et seq.** — Interest rate general provisions

### Bond Requirements
- **$25,000 surety bond** filed with the SCC BFI
- Bond must be issued by a company authorized to do business in Virginia
- Bond must name the Commonwealth of Virginia as obligee
- Bond must be maintained for the entire license period
- Failure to maintain bond may result in license suspension

## Compliance Considerations

### Advertising & Marketing
- All advertisements must comply with Va. Code § 6.2-1820 and federal Truth in Lending Act (TILA)
- APR must be clearly disclosed
- No misleading or deceptive representations

### Collections
- Must comply with Virginia Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (Va. Code § 6.2-2200 et seq.)
- No harassment, threats, or unfair practices
- Written validation notices required

### Privacy & Data Security
- Must comply with Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (CDPA)
- Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) privacy rules apply
- Safeguarding Rule compliance required

### Recordkeeping
- Maintain complete records of all loans for at least 3 years after final payment
- Records must be available for examination by the BFI upon request
- Electronic records are permissible if retrievable in readable format

### Examinations
- BFI conducts regular examinations (typically every 12–24 months)
- Examinations cover: loan portfolio quality, compliance, financial condition, internal controls
- Examination reports are confidential

# PalmFi — Texas Regulated Lender License Research

## Overview

Texas OCCC (Office of Consumer Credit Commissioner) regulates non-depository
lenders making loans at >10% interest. Since PalmFi's rates will be 18-29% APR,
**a Regulated Lender License under Chapter 342, Texas Finance Code is required**.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Create an ALECS Account
- **ALECS** = Automated Licensing and Electronic filing System
- Register at `occc.texas.gov/alecs`
- This is the portal for licensing, renewals, and annual reports

### Step 2: Prepare Application Materials
You'll need:
1. **Business Information** — LLC name, EIN, physical address, registered agent
2. **Principal/Owner Information** — Background check + credit report for each owner
3. **Business Plan** — Your lending model, target market, underwriting methodology
4. **Financial Statements** — Balance sheet, operating budget, capital plan
5. **Surety Bond** — $25,000-$100,000 (TBD by OCCC based on loan volume)
6. **Proposed Loan Contracts** — Must comply with TFC Chapter 342 plain language rules
7. **Compliance Plan** — How you'll handle: consumer complaints, data privacy, collections
8. **Software Review** — OCCC reviews your lending software for compliance

### Step 3: Fees
- **Application Fee:** ~$1,000 (one-time)
- **Surety Bond:** $500-$2,000/yr (varies by bond amount and your credit)
- **Annual Renewal:** ~$300-500 (due Nov 1 - Dec 31)
- **Annual Report:** Due May 1 each year

### Timeline
- ALECS account setup: 1 day
- Application preparation: 1-2 weeks
- OCCC review: 4-6 weeks (can be expedited)
- **Total: ~6-8 weeks**

## Alternative: Partner with Existing Licensee

Instead of getting your own license (faster, cheaper):
1. Find a Texas-regulated lender willing to white-label your platform
2. PalmFi operates as their "service provider" under their license
3. They take 10-30% of loan revenue as license fee
4. You handle origination, underwriting, servicing

**Where to find partners:**
- Fintech networking events (LendIt Fintech, Fintech Nexus)
- Online lender marketplaces
- Direct outreach to small/medium Texas lenders

## Key Compliance Requirements (Post-License)

Once licensed:
- Contracts must be in **plain language** (TAC Chapter 90)
- Maximum interest rates defined by TFC Chapter 342
- Must provide **TILA disclosures** (Federal Regulation Z)
- **Annual report** due May 1 through ALECS
- **Record keeping**: 2+ years of all loan records
- **Collection practices** must comply with Texas Debt Collection Act
- **Privacy** must comply with GLBA (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act)

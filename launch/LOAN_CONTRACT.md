## 4. Prepayment Terms

**You can pay off this loan early at any time with no penalty.**

- **No Prepayment Penalty:** Texas law (Texas Finance Code § 342.252) and this agreement do not charge any fee or penalty for paying off your loan early — whether you pay a little extra each month or pay the entire balance at once.
- **How Early Payoff Works:** If you pay off the loan before the end of the term, you only pay:
  - The remaining principal balance
  - Any interest that has accrued (built up) since your last payment
  - Any unpaid fees (such as late fees if applicable)
- **No Rebate of Unearned Interest (Precomputed Loans):** If your loan uses precomputed interest, you are entitled to a refund of any unearned finance charge under the "actuarial method" as required by Texas Finance Code § 342.259.
- **How to Prepay:** Contact us at [Phone Number] or [Email Address] to request a payoff statement. We will provide you with the exact amount needed to pay off the loan within [Number, e.g., 3] business days.
- **Extra Payments:** You may also make extra payments in addition to your regular monthly payment at any time. Any extra payment will be applied to your principal balance first, which reduces the total interest you pay over the life of the loan.

## 6. Default & Collections

**What counts as a "default":** You are considered in default if any of the following happen:
1. You fail to make a payment when it is due and do not pay it within [Number, e.g., 30] days after we send you a written notice
2. You provide false or misleading information on your loan application
3. You file for bankruptcy or become insolvent
4. You die (in which case your estate becomes responsible for the remaining balance)

**What we will do before collections start:**
- We will send you written notices at least [Number, e.g., 30] days before taking any collection action
- We will try to contact you by phone and mail to work out a payment arrangement
- We will follow all rules of the Texas Debt Collection Act (Texas Finance Code Chapter 392)

**What may happen if you default:**
1. **Late fees** will continue to apply to missed payments as described in Section 5
2. **Accrued interest** continues to build on the unpaid principal balance
3. We may **accelerate the loan**, meaning the entire remaining balance (principal + accrued interest + allowed fees) becomes due immediately
4. We may **report your default to credit reporting agencies** (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion), which could negatively affect your credit score
5. We may **hire a collection agency** to collect the debt
6. We may **file a lawsuit** to obtain a court judgment against you

**Our collection promise:** We will:
- Not call you before 8:00 AM or after 9:00 PM your local time
- Not use threats, harassment, or abusive language
- Not contact you at work if you tell us your employer prohibits it
- Provide you with verification of the debt if you request it in writing within 30 days of our first contact
- Follow all applicable federal and state collection laws

**Right to Cure:** You have the right to cure (fix) a default after receiving a notice of default. If you pay the full amount past due (including late fees) within [Number, e.g., 30] days of the notice, the default is cured and the loan continues as if no default happened.

## 7. Borrower Rights

As a borrower, you have important legal rights. Here are the key ones:

### a) Right to Receive Disclosures
You are entitled to receive clear, written disclosures about your loan terms before signing, including the APR, finance charge, payment schedule, and total cost of the loan. These are provided in this agreement and in a separate Truth in Lending Disclosure statement.

### b) Right to Cancel (Rescission)
**For loans secured by your home:** If this loan is secured by a lien on your primary residence, you have the right to cancel this agreement within 3 business days of signing. We will notify you separately if this applies.

**For unsecured personal loans:** There is generally no federal right to cancel. However, if you change your mind, contact us immediately — we may allow cancellation if the funds have not yet been disbursed.

### c) Right to Prepay Without Penalty
As described in Section 4, you may pay off this loan early at any time with no penalty.

### d) Right to Dispute Errors
You have the right to dispute any billing errors or charges you believe are incorrect. To dispute:
- Send a written notice to [Dispute Address / Email] within 60 days of the statement showing the error
- Include your name, loan number, and a description of the error
- We must investigate and respond within 30 days (or 90 days under certain circumstances)
- During the investigation, you do not have to pay the disputed amount

### e) Right to Receive Payoff Statement
You may request a written statement showing the exact amount needed to pay off your loan at any time. We will provide this within [Number, e.g., 3] business days of your request, free of charge once per loan term.

### f) Right to Privacy
Your personal and financial information will be protected in accordance with the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) and our Privacy Policy. We will not share your information with third parties except as necessary to service your loan or as required by law.

### g) Right to Be Free from Discrimination
Federal and Texas law prohibit credit discrimination based on race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age (provided you are old enough to enter a contract), or because you receive public assistance income. If you believe you have been discriminated against, contact:
- **Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB):** 1-855-411-2372 or consumerfinance.gov
- **Texas Office of Consumer Credit Commissioner (OCCC):** 1-800-538-1579 or occc.texas.gov

### h) Right to Information About This Loan
You may request a copy of your signed loan agreement, payment history, and amortization schedule at any time. We will provide these documents at no charge.

### i) Right to Complain
If you have a complaint about this loan, our service, or our collection practices, you may:
1. **Contact us directly:** [Complaint Email] or [Complaint Phone Number]
2. **File a complaint with the Texas OCCC:** occc.texas.gov / 1-800-538-1579
3. **File a complaint with the CFPB:** consumerfinance.gov / 1-855-411-2372

### j) Right to Waive Certain Rights Only in Writing
Any waiver of your rights under this agreement must be in writing and signed by you. Verbal agreements to change loan terms are not binding.

## 8. Lender Rights

### a) Right to Receive Payment
The Lender has the right to receive all payments described in this agreement on time, including principal, interest, and any applicable fees.

### b) Right to Verify Information
The Lender has the right to verify the information you provided in your loan application, including:
- Obtaining your credit report from one or more credit bureaus
- Verifying your income and employment
- Confirming your identity

A credit report inquiry may appear on your credit file.

### c) Right to Assign or Sell the Loan
The Lender may sell, transfer, or assign this loan agreement to another company at any time. If this happens:
- The new company takes over all the Lender's rights and responsibilities under this agreement
- You will receive written notice of the transfer
- Your payment terms will not change
- All your rights as a borrower (Section 7) remain in effect
- The new company must comply with all applicable laws

### d) Right to Take Legal Action
If you default on this loan and do not cure the default as described in Section 6, the Lender has the right to take legal action against you to recover the amount owed. This includes filing a lawsuit in a Texas court to obtain a judgment.

### e) Right to Charge Fees Allowed by Law
The Lender may charge fees specifically allowed by this agreement and Texas law, including:
- Late fees (Section 5)
- Returned payment fees (Section 5)
- Attorney's fees and court costs if collection requires legal action (as allowed by Texas law)

### f) Right to Modify Automatic Payment Instructions
The Lender may change the way automatic payments are processed, with reasonable notice to you, as long as the change does not increase your payment amount or change the due date.

### g) No Right to Garnish Certain Wages
The Lender acknowledges that certain types of income are exempt from garnishment under federal and Texas law, including Social Security benefits, disability benefits, child support, and certain pension benefits.

📐 Methodology Description

Bayesian Improved Surname Geocoding (BISG):

The BISG method estimates the probability that an applicant belongs to a particular racial or ethnic group by combining two sources of information:

  • Surname analysis: P(race | surname) — derived from U.S. Census Bureau surname frequency data, which provides the racial/ethnic distribution associated with each surname.
  • Geographic analysis: P(race | tract) — derived from Census tract-level demographic data (ACS 5-year estimates), representing the racial composition of the applicant's neighborhood.

The two probabilities are combined using Bayes' rule:

P(race | surname, tract) ∝ P(race | surname) × P(race | tract)

4/5th Rule (Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection Procedures):

An adverse impact ratio is computed for each group as the group's approval rate divided by the approval rate of the highest-performing group. An AIR below 0.80 (80%) constitutes evidence of potential disparate impact under the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (Regulation B).

Proxy Feature Detection: Continuous features are normalized to [0, 1], and group means are compared to the overall mean. A deviation exceeding 0.30 flags the feature as a potential proxy for protected characteristics.

Limitations: BISG estimates are probabilistic and subject to misclassification error. Results should be validated against actual demographic data where available. The analysis identifies statistical disparities, not legal causation.
